Research Focus

Physical Mechanisms of Chromatin Organisation Across Length Scales
Current postdoctoral work
  • Develop theoretical and computational models to study nucleosome dynamics, focusing on how epigenetic modifications and pioneer transcription factors reshape chromatin accessibility and stability.
  • Investigate large-scale chromatin reorganisation in sperm cells to understand the physical principles underlying genome compaction and toroidal structure formation.
Physical Mechanisms Underlying Nucleosome Retention in Spermiogenesis
Doctoral research
  • Developed an analytical framework for computing sequence-dependent nucleosome free energies, including enthalpic and entropic contributions, based on a rigid base-pair model of DNA.
  • Proposed a mechanistic explanation for selective nucleosome retention in sperm by combining nucleosome energetics with cooperative protamine binding in a stochastic model.
  • Studied how transcript architecture and amino-acid choice contribute to nucleosome positioning across eukaryotes.
